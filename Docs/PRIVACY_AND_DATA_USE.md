# DIR DIVING Privacy and Data Use

Last updated: 2026-05-25

## Overview

DIR DIVING stores and processes dive-related information to support logging, preliminary planning review, watch/phone synchronization, and user reference material.

## Data that may be stored

Depending on the features you use, the app may store:

- dive sessions and samples
- timestamps and dive duration
- depth and temperature samples
- entry and exit GPS points when available
- manual dive notes and equipment fields
- planner inputs
- selected language and unit preferences
- watch/iPhone sync state and conflict data
- user-selected images sent to Apple Watch

## Data location

Data may be stored locally on device and, when enabled and available, mirrored through Apple services used by the app such as iCloud key-value storage or CloudKit-related capabilities configured for the project.

## Sync behavior

When Watch/iPhone sync is active, dive sessions, deletes, settings context, or reference images may be transferred between the paired devices.

## Permissions

The app may request access to:

- location, to handle dive entry/exit GPS points when available
- photo library, to let the iPhone send reference images to Apple Watch

If permissions are denied, related features may be limited or unavailable.

## Safety note

Sensor and sync data can be incomplete, delayed, unavailable, or inaccurate depending on hardware, permissions, environmental conditions, and connectivity.

## Exports

When you export dive data, you are responsible for how those files are shared, stored, and used outside the app.

## No sale of personal data statement

This repository does not describe any in-app advertising sale flow or third-party data marketplace behavior. Any future production privacy policy should be reviewed before public release.
